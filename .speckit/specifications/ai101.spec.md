- AI = systems that learn patterns from data to make predictions or generate outputs
- Different from traditional software (explicit rules) → AI learns from examples
- AI can assist with language, images, speech, forecasting, and recommendations

- Generative AI applications combine models with data, security, monitoring, and lifecycle operations (LLMOps).

## [two-column] Strengths & Weaknesses of AI

**Left**:
- Photographic memory across internet-scale data
- Tireless: processes thousands of pages in seconds
- Multilingual: dozens of languages
- Pattern recognition humans would miss
- Consistent: no bad days or distractions

**Right**:
- No common sense or real-world understanding
- Prompt-dependent: garbage in, garbage out
- Confidently wrong: fabricates with conviction
- Mimics reasoning but fails simple logic
- Blind to context it wasn't given

**Notes**: This is the 'honest conversation' slide. AI has genuine superpowers and genuine blind spots—understanding both is what separates productive use from disappointment.

Expand on each bullet:
- Photographic memory: trained on the entire corpus of cleaned-up internet data
- Tireless and instant: can process thousands of pages in seconds without fatigue
- Multilingual: can read, write, and translate across dozens of languages
- Pattern recognition at scale: spots correlations humans would miss across massive datasets
- Consistent: doesn't have bad days, distractions, or office politics
- No common sense: doesn't truly understand the world the way humans do
- Must be told exactly what to do: quality of output depends entirely on quality of the prompt

--- Supplemental (from ContentUrls) ---

- **Chat is reactive; agents are action-oriented.** Unlike chat experiences that generate responses, agents can reason over a request and *take autonomous actions* by calling tools, accessing data, and making multi-step decisions — which increases both power and risk.  
  Source: https://learn.microsoft.com/azure/ai-services/agents/overview

- **Agents are built to operate inside enterprise systems.** Agent platforms manage identity, authentication, and secure tool access (for example, API calls and data sources), reinforcing why governance and guardrails are essential as autonomy increases.  
  Source: https://learn.microsoft.com/azure/ai-services/agents/overview

- **Copilot agents are grounded in organizational data and permissions.** When agents work with Microsoft 365 data, they only surface information users are authorized to access, highlighting permissioning as a core control mechanism.  
  Source: https://learn.microsoft.com/microsoft-365-copilot/microsoft-365-copilot-overview

- **Observability becomes critical as autonomy grows.** Agent services provide end-to-end tracing and monitoring so teams can inspect decisions and tool calls — a capability far less critical for simple chat, but essential for agents.  
  Source: https://learn.microsoft.com/azure/ai-services/agents/overview
